Project Overview

Building sustainable cities through gameplay

Carbonbusters is an innovative city transformation game developed for the IndieCade Climate Jam, where players tackle the challenge of reducing transportation emissions while gaining political support for sustainable infrastructure changes.

The game empowers players to transform car-dependent cities by implementing progressive changes—from adding bike lanes to replacing highways with efficient rail systems. Through strategic decision-making and resource management, players experience the real-world complexities of urban planning and environmental policy.

Built with advanced graph data structures for realistic pathfinding and agent-based systems, Carbonbusters demonstrates how gaming can effectively communicate complex environmental challenges while providing an engaging and educational experience.

Game Features

City Transformation

Complete urban planning system allowing players to redesign cities with sustainable transportation infrastructure.

Advanced Pathfinding

Sophisticated graph data structures enable realistic agent-based navigation between infrastructure nodes.

Real-Time Impact

Dynamic feedback system showing immediate environmental impact of infrastructure decisions and policy changes.

Political Strategy

Engage with citizens and gain political support to implement progressive environmental policies.

Technical Challenges

Graph Algorithm Optimization

Implementing efficient pathfinding algorithms to handle complex transportation networks with hundreds of interconnected nodes while maintaining smooth gameplay performance.

Agent-Based Modeling

Creating realistic citizen behavior patterns that respond dynamically to infrastructure changes and policy decisions in real-time.

Data Visualization

Designing intuitive visual representations of complex environmental data to help players understand the impact of their decisions.
