IMPACT Grant Project, 2024

Complete website redesign for the LGBTQ+ library to increase community engagement and donations. Modern, accessible design with improved user experience through comprehensive research and usability testing.

Category Web Development
Role Lead Developer & Designer
Duration 3 Months
Team 5 Members

Overview

This IMPACT grant project represents a collaborative effort with the Gerber/Hart Library & Archives to revolutionize their digital presence and strengthen their connection with Chicago's LGBTQ+ community.

Through comprehensive user research, UX design, and web development, our interdisciplinary team focused on creating a more accessible and engaging platform. The primary goals included increasing website traffic, boosting donations, and enhancing the visibility of the library's invaluable LGBTQ+ historical resources.

By conducting targeted user interviews, creating interactive prototypes, and performing extensive usability testing, we delivered a user-centered digital experience that authentically represents Gerber/Hart's mission.

Key Features

Community Engagement

Increased website traffic and user engagement to better serve Chicago's LGBTQ+ community through improved digital presence.

Donation Growth

Streamlined donation process and increased funding to support the library's mission and operations.

Resource Visibility

Enhanced discoverability of LGBTQ+ historical archives and cultural resources for researchers and community members.

Accessibility Standards

Implemented modern WCAG accessibility standards to ensure the website is usable by all community members.
